## Station Overview

Every Hyrox race features the same 8 functional workout stations, performed in the same order. Between each station, you run 1km. Here's your complete guide to every station.

> **Want a deeper dive?** Our [Station Masterclass blog series](/blog/series/station-masterclass/) breaks down each station (plus the 8km of running) in a full, 2,000-word guide, technique, training drills, race-day rep scheme, and the mistakes that cost first-timers the most time.

## Station 1: SkiErg, 1,000m

The SkiErg is a Concept2 machine that simulates cross-country skiing. You pull two handles downward in a rhythmic motion, driving power from your lats, core, and triceps.

**What makes it tough:** It's the first station, so the temptation is to go too hard. The SkiErg is deceptively tiring, your heart rate spikes fast.

**Pro tips:**
- Use a **rhythmic, hip-hinge** motion, bend at the hips and pull through
- Don't death-grip the handles; keep your grip relaxed
- Aim for **consistent split times** rather than going all-out
- Target pace: 1:50-2:10/500m for most athletes

| Level | Men | Women |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Elite | 3:15–3:30 | 3:45–4:00 |
| Competitive | 3:45–4:15 | 4:15–4:45 |
| Average | 4:15–5:00 | 4:45–5:30 |
| Beginner | 5:00–6:30 | 5:30–7:00 |

**Deep dive:** [Hyrox SkiErg, How Not to Blow Your Race in the First 4 Minutes](/blog/hyrox-skierg-technique-pacing/).

## Station 2: Sled Push, 50m

You push a weighted sled across 50 meters of smooth floor. The weights vary by division:

| Category | Men | Women |
|----------|-----|-------|
| Open | 152 kg | 102 kg |
| Pro | 202 kg | 152 kg |

Sled weights are total mass including the sled itself, per the [official Hyrox Singles Rulebook](https://hyrox.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/25_26-Singles-Rulebook_en_R1.pdf).

**What makes it tough:** Pure leg strength and willpower. The sled can feel impossibly heavy if you lose momentum.

**Pro tips:**
- Keep your body at a **45-degree angle:** lean into the sled
- Drive with your **legs**, not your upper body
- Take **short, powerful steps** rather than long strides
- Whatever you do, **don't stop**. Restarting a sled from zero is brutally hard
- Breathe rhythmically through the push

| Level | Men | Women |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Elite | 1:15–1:30 | 1:30–1:45 |
| Competitive | 1:45–2:30 | 2:00–2:45 |
| Average | 2:30–3:30 | 2:45–4:00 |
| Beginner | 3:30–5:00+ | 4:00–6:00+ |

**Deep dive:** [Hyrox Sled Push, The Station That Stops First-Timers Cold](/blog/hyrox-sled-push-technique/).

**Standards page:** [Hyrox Sled Push weight by division & rules](/stations/sled-push/standards/).

## Station 3: Sled Pull, 50m

Grab a rope attached to a weighted sled and pull it toward you, hand over hand, for 50m. The sled pull uses lighter weights than the sled push.

| Category | Men | Women |
|----------|-----|-------|
| Open | 78 kg | 48 kg |
| Pro | 118 kg | 78 kg |

**What makes it tough:** Grip endurance is the limiter for most athletes. Your forearms and biceps fatigue fast.

**Pro tips:**
- Sit low in a **wide squat stance** for leverage
- Pull with your **back and legs**, not just your arms
- Use a **hand-over-hand rhythm:** don't rush, stay smooth
- Wear gloves or use chalk if allowed (check event rules)
- Keep the rope tension constant, don't let it go slack

| Level | Men | Women |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Elite | 1:15–1:30 | 1:30–1:45 |
| Competitive | 1:45–2:30 | 2:00–2:45 |
| Average | 2:30–3:30 | 2:45–4:00 |
| Beginner | 3:30–5:00+ | 4:00–5:30+ |

**Deep dive:** [Hyrox Sled Pull, Stance, Rope, and the Hand-Over-Hand Mistake](/blog/hyrox-sled-pull-technique/).

**Standards page:** [Hyrox Sled Pull weight by division & rules](/stations/sled-pull/standards/).

## Station 4: Burpee Broad Jumps, 80m

The most feared station. Perform a burpee, then immediately broad jump forward. Repeat for 80 meters.

**What makes it tough:** This is universally considered the hardest Hyrox station. The combination of getting up and down from the floor with jumping forward is brutally taxing on your cardiovascular system and legs.

**Pro tips:**
- **Pace yourself:** this station is a war of attrition
- Use a **step-back burpee** instead of jumping back (saves energy)
- Your broad jumps don't need to be huge, **consistency beats distance**
- Focus on your breathing, exhale on the jump
- Don't look at how far you have to go; focus on one rep at a time

| Level | Men | Women |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Elite | 2:45–3:15 | 3:15–3:45 |
| Competitive | 3:30–4:30 | 4:00–5:00 |
| Average | 4:30–6:00 | 5:00–6:30 |
| Beginner | 6:00–9:00+ | 6:30–10:00+ |

This is where most time is won or lost. An elite athlete can save 3+ minutes over a beginner here.

**Deep dive:** [Hyrox Burpee Broad Jumps, The Race-Maker Station](/blog/hyrox-burpee-broad-jumps-strategy/).

## Station 5: Rowing, 1,000m

The Concept2 rower. A full-body exercise that tests your aerobic capacity, leg power, and mental endurance.

**What makes it tough:** Coming after burpee broad jumps, your legs are already cooked. Rowing demands a strong leg drive, which is hard when you're fatigued.

**Pro tips:**
- Focus on the **drive sequence**: legs → back → arms
- Don't pull with your arms first, power comes from your **legs**
- Keep your stroke rate moderate: **24–28 strokes per minute**
- Target split: 1:50–2:15/500m for most athletes
- Strap in quickly, seconds count

| Level | Men | Women |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Elite | 3:10–3:30 | 3:40–4:00 |
| Competitive | 3:30–4:00 | 4:00–4:30 |
| Average | 4:00–4:45 | 4:30–5:15 |
| Beginner | 4:45–6:00+ | 5:15–6:30+ |

**Deep dive:** [Hyrox Rowing, How to Recover Without Losing Time](/blog/hyrox-rowing-technique/).

**Standards page:** [Hyrox Rowing distance, machine & rules](/stations/rowing/standards/).

## Station 6: Farmers Carry, 200m

Carry two heavy kettlebells (one in each hand) for 200 meters around a marked course.

| Category | Men | Women |
|----------|-----|-------|
| Open | 2×24 kg | 2×16 kg |
| Pro | 2×32 kg | 2×24 kg |

**What makes it tough:** Grip failure. After the sled pull and rowing, your forearms and hands are spent. Plus, 200m is a long way carrying heavy weight.

**Pro tips:**
- **Grip strength is king:** train it before race day
- Walk at a **brisk, steady pace:** running wastes energy and risks dropping
- Keep your shoulders back and core braced
- If you must set them down, do it quickly and pick back up immediately
- Use **hook grip** if you can tolerate it

| Level | Men | Women |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Elite | 1:45–2:00 | 2:00–2:15 |
| Competitive | 2:15–3:00 | 2:30–3:15 |
| Average | 3:00–4:00 | 3:15–4:30 |
| Beginner | 4:00–6:00+ | 4:30–6:30+ |

**Deep dive:** [Hyrox Farmers Carry + Sandbag Lunges, Grip, Legs, and the Point Where You're Allowed to Cry](/blog/hyrox-farmers-carry-sandbag-lunges/).

## Station 7: Sandbag Lunges, 100m

Carry a sandbag on your shoulder and lunge for 100 meters. Alternate legs with each step.

| Category | Men | Women |
|----------|-----|-------|
| Open | 20 kg | 10 kg |
| Pro | 30 kg | 20 kg |

**What makes it tough:** Your quads are destroyed from everything before. Lunging with a sandbag on your shoulder for 100 meters is a quad-burning, balance-testing nightmare.

**Pro tips:**
- Take **shorter steps:** deep lunges waste energy
- Your back knee should **lightly touch** the ground (it's required)
- Switch the sandbag shoulder halfway to balance fatigue
- Keep your torso **upright:** don't lean forward
- Find a rhythm and don't stop

| Level | Men | Women |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Elite | 2:45–3:15 | 3:15–3:45 |
| Competitive | 3:30–4:30 | 4:00–5:00 |
| Average | 4:30–6:00 | 5:00–6:30 |
| Beginner | 6:00–8:00+ | 6:30–9:00+ |

**Deep dive:** [Hyrox Farmers Carry + Sandbag Lunges, Grip, Legs, and the Point Where You're Allowed to Cry](/blog/hyrox-farmers-carry-sandbag-lunges/).

**Standards page:** [Hyrox Sandbag Lunges weight by division & no-reps](/stations/sandbag-lunges/standards/).

## Station 8: Wall Balls, 100 reps

The final station. Perform a squat and throw a medicine ball to a target height, catch it, and repeat. Per the current Hyrox Singles Rulebook, **all divisions complete 100 reps** — the older 75-rep women's standard is no longer used.

| Category | Reps | Ball Weight | Target Height |
|----------|------|-------------|---------------|
| Men Open | 100 | 6 kg | 3.0m / 10ft |
| Men Pro | 100 | 9 kg | 3.0m / 10ft |
| Women Open | 100 | 4 kg | 2.7m / 9ft |
| Women Pro | 100 | 6 kg | 2.7m / 9ft |

**What makes it tough:** 100 reps when you're completely gassed. Your legs, shoulders, and lungs are all screaming. But the finish line is right after this.

**Pro tips:**
- Break it into **sets** (e.g., 25-25-25-25 or 20-20-20-15)
- Use your **legs** to drive the ball up, not your arms
- Stand close to the wall, less distance to throw
- Keep a **steady rhythm:** don't sprint the first 30 and die
- Remember: this is the last thing between you and the finish line

| Level | Men (100 reps) | Women (100 reps) |
|-------|----------------|------------------|
| Elite | 3:00–3:30 | 3:15–3:45 |
| Competitive | 3:45–4:30 | 4:00–4:45 |
| Average | 4:30–6:00 | 4:45–6:00 |
| Beginner | 6:00–8:00+ | 6:00–7:30+ |

**Deep dive:** [Hyrox Wall Balls, The Final Boss](/blog/hyrox-wall-balls-technique/).

**Standards page:** [Hyrox Wall Balls reps, weight & target height](/stations/wall-balls/standards/).

## Where to Focus Your Training

The biggest time differences between levels are in:

1. **Burpee Broad Jumps:** up to 4+ minute gap between elite and beginner
2. **Sandbag Lunges:** 4+ minute gap, heavily affected by fatigue
3. **Sled Push/Pull:** 3+ minute gap, pure strength dependent
4. **Running:** 17+ minute gap across the 8 segments
