## Before Race Day

### What to Bring

Pack your bag the night before. Here's the essential checklist:

**Must-haves:**
- Race confirmation / QR code (on your phone or printed)
- Photo ID
- Running shoes (see our [shoe recommendations](/gear/shoes/))
- Comfortable workout clothing
- Water bottle
- Post-race change of clothes
- Towel
- Energy gels or snacks for before/during

**Optional but recommended:**
- Grip gloves or chalk (check if venue allows it)
- Knee sleeves (helpful for lunges and wall balls)
- Foam roller or massage gun for warm-up
- Headband or sweatband
- Spare socks
- Flip-flops for post-race

### Nutrition: The Day Before

- Eat a **carb-rich dinner** the night before (pasta, rice, potatoes)
- Stay well hydrated throughout the day
- Avoid heavy, fatty, or spicy foods
- Avoid alcohol
- Don't try any new foods — stick to what you know

### Nutrition: Race Morning

Eat **2–3 hours before** your start time:
- Oatmeal with banana and honey
- Toast with peanut butter
- Rice cakes with jam
- A small smoothie

**30 minutes before:**
- A caffeine gel or coffee if you're used to it
- A few sips of water — don't over-hydrate

## Arriving at the Venue

### Timing

Arrive **at least 90 minutes** before your wave start time. You'll need time for:
- Finding parking / getting to the venue
- Checking in and collecting your bib
- Dropping bags
- Warming up
- Getting to the start corral

### Check-in Process

1. Show your confirmation and ID at the registration desk
2. Collect your **bib number** and timing chip
3. Pin your bib to the front of your shirt (4 pins, one at each corner)
4. Attach the timing chip to your shoe
5. Drop your bag at the designated bag drop area

### Warm-Up (15–20 minutes)

Start your warm-up 20 minutes before your wave:

1. **5 min light jog** or jump rope
2. **Dynamic stretches:** leg swings, arm circles, hip openers
3. **Activation:** 10 air squats, 10 push-ups, 10 lunges
4. **Short rowing or SkiErg** if practice machines are available
5. **2–3 practice broad jumps** to dial in your technique
6. **Mental prep:** visualize the race, review your pacing plan

## During the Race

### Pacing Strategy

The biggest mistake first-timers make is **going out too fast**. Here's a smarter approach:

**The Negative Split Strategy:**
- Runs 1–4: Run at **70–75%** effort. These should feel comfortable.
- Runs 5–6: Run at **80%** effort. You're in the grind now.
- Runs 7–8: Run at **85–90%** effort. Leave everything on the course.

**Station Pacing:**
- Stations 1–4: Steady and controlled. Don't sprint the SkiErg.
- Stations 5–6: Maintain effort but expect some slowdown.
- Station 7 (Lunges): This is where races are often lost. Stay disciplined.
- Station 8 (Wall Balls): Break into manageable sets. The finish line is close.

### Transition Tips

The time between finishing a run and starting a station (and vice versa) is called a **transition**. Experienced racers save minutes here:

- **Don't walk** through the transition zone — keep jogging
- Know which station is next so you can mentally prepare during the run
- At the station, set up quickly (strap into rower/SkiErg immediately)
- Have a **3-second rule**: once you arrive, start within 3 seconds

### Hydration During the Race

- There are usually water stations on the running course
- Take a few sips — don't chug
- Some athletes carry a small flask or gel for mid-race fuel
- An energy gel between stations 4–5 can help (this is roughly the midpoint)

### Mental Game

- **Break the race into quarters:** Stations 1–2, 3–4, 5–6, 7–8
- Focus only on the current station, not what's coming
- Use the runs to **recover mentally** — zone out, listen to the crowd
- When it gets hard (station 4–6 is the dark zone), remember: everyone else is hurting too
- Count reps, focus on breathing, find a rhythm

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Starting too fast** — the #1 mistake. Control your adrenaline.
2. **Skipping warm-up** — cold muscles + heavy sleds = slow times and injury risk
3. **Wearing new shoes** — always race in shoes you've trained in
4. **Ignoring grip training** — grip failure on the sled pull and farmers carry will cost you minutes
5. **Poor nutrition** — don't try new foods or skip your pre-race meal
6. **Not practicing transitions** — those 30-second walks between stations add up
7. **Going all-out on wall balls too early** — pace your final station
8. **Forgetting to enjoy it** — Hyrox is supposed to be fun. Soak in the atmosphere!

## After the Race

### Recovery

- **Keep moving** — walk around for 10–15 minutes post-finish
- **Hydrate** with electrolytes
- **Eat within 30 minutes** — protein + carbs (protein shake, banana, recovery bar)
- **Stretch** major muscle groups: quads, hamstrings, shoulders, hip flexors
- **Cold shower or ice bath** within a few hours if possible

### Check Your Results

Results are posted on:
- The **Hyrox app** (usually within 1–2 hours)
- The **Hyrox website** results page
- Screens at the event venue

You'll get splits for every run and every station, plus your overall time and ranking.
