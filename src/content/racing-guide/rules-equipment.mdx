Hyrox has a remarkably **short rulebook** for a global sport. The format is standardised, the weights are fixed, the running distance is fixed, and the athlete-equipment list is mostly "wear what you want, don't break venue floors."

That short rulebook leaves a lot of grey area. Below is the practical rule-by-rule breakdown — what's technically legal, what's actually used by top finishers, and what's banned.

For a deeper dive on each topic, follow the linked posts.

## What's banned

| Equipment | Banned? | Why |
|-----------|---------|-----|
| **Lifting straps** | ❌ Yes | Removes grip from the equation |
| **Lifting hooks** | ❌ Yes | Mechanical grip aid |
| **Figure-8 straps** | ❌ Yes | Same as lifting straps |
| **Tacky-coated palm gloves** | ❌ Yes | Bonds hand to equipment |
| **Carabiners or clips** | ❌ Yes | Locks hand to rope/handle |
| **Track spikes / cleats** | ❌ Yes | Damages venue floors |
| **Open-toed shoes** | ❌ Yes | Safety |
| **Outside assistance during race** | ❌ Yes | No coaches/family handing equipment mid-race |
| **Headphones / earbuds** | ❌ Yes | Safety + judging communication |
| **Outdoor shoes (visibly dirty)** | ⚠️ Discretional | Some venues turn athletes away |

## The general principle

Hyrox's rule philosophy boils down to one sentence:

> **Anything that improves friction (chalk, tape, gloves) is legal. Anything that augments grip mechanically (locks the hand to equipment) is illegal.**

This is the cleanest way to think about grey-area equipment. If in doubt, ask the head judge before your heat starts. They'll always tell you.

## Station-by-station equipment cheatsheet

| Station | Useful equipment | Skip |
|---------|------------------|------|
| **SkiErg** | None | Gloves (slippery on handles) |
| **Sled push** | Stable shoes | Gloves, chalk |
| **Sled pull** | Chalk, tape, gloves | Lifting straps (banned) |
| **Burpee broad jumps** | Light, breathable kit | Anything chunky around the wrists |
| **Rowing** | Optional chalk | Gloves (rarely needed) |
| **Farmers carry** | Chalk (provided) | Lifting hooks (banned) |
| **Sandbag lunges** | Tape on shoulders if it chafes | Heavy belt |
| **Wall balls** | Light chalk | Gloves (catches) |

## Apparel rules — the short list

- Race bib must remain visible on the front torso
- Closed-toe athletic shoes
- No headphones or earbuds
- No outdoor shoes (clean shoes only)

That's it. Otherwise wear whatever you train in.

## Pre-race kit checklist

For your first Hyrox, this is the minimum kit:

1. **Race shoes you've trained in for 6+ weeks** (not new)
2. **Singlet or fitted t-shirt** (not cotton)
3. **Shorts or leggings** (fitted, no flapping)
4. **Liquid chalk** (50-100 ml bottle, in your gear bag)
5. **Athletic tape** (pre-cut 4-inch strips for palms if needed)
6. **Energy gels** (1-2 small gels in a belt or pocket)
7. **Heart-rate monitor** (optional but useful for pacing)
8. **A backup pair of laces** (in case one breaks)

For repeat racers:

- Add anything you've trained with that worked
- Replace anything you've trained with that didn't work
- **Don't add new gear on race day**

## Getting a rule clarified

If you're unsure whether something is legal at a specific event:

1. **Email the event organiser** before race week (response time: 1-3 days)
2. **Ask the head judge** during athlete check-in
3. **Check the latest hyrox.com athlete handbook** — published before each season

Hyrox's rules are stable year to year, but specific events can have local variations (venue rules, sponsor restrictions). Always check the event-specific athlete brief.
