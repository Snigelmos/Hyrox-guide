## Your First Hyrox: 8-Week Plan

This program assumes you can run 5 km and have basic gym experience. By week 8, you'll be ready to complete your first Hyrox race confidently.

**Training schedule:** 4 sessions per week (2 running, 2 gym/functional)

---

## Program Overview

| Week | Focus | Running Volume | Gym Sessions |
|------|-------|----------------|--------------|
| 1–2 | Foundation | 3×3 km | 2× station practice |
| 3–4 | Build | 3×4 km | 2× station combos |
| 5–6 | Race Simulation | 2×5 km + intervals | 2× race simulation |
| 7 | Peak | Race simulation | Taper gym work |
| 8 | Race Week | Light running only | Rest & prep |

**Day 1, Easy Run**
- 3 km at conversational pace (you can talk while running)
- Focus on form: upright posture, relaxed shoulders, mid-foot strike

**Day 2, Station Introduction A**
- 10 min warm-up (jog + dynamic stretching)
- **SkiErg:** 3×500 m with 2 min rest (learn the movement)
- **Sled Push:** 5×25 m at light weight (focus on body angle)
- **Rowing:** 3×500 m with 2 min rest (practice drive sequence)
- **Wall Balls:** 3×15 reps with 1 min rest

**Day 3, Run + Strength**
- 3 km easy run
- Then: 3 rounds of 10 squats, 10 push-ups, 10 lunges each leg, 30 s plank

**Day 4, Station Introduction B**
- 10 min warm-up
- **Sled Pull:** 5×25 m at light weight (learn hand-over-hand technique)
- **Burpee Broad Jumps:** 3×20 m (focus on efficient movement)
- **Farmers Carry:** 3×50 m with moderate weight
- **Sandbag Lunges:** 3×20 m with light sandbag

**Day 2, Station Combos A**
- **Combo 1:** 500 m SkiErg → 400 m run → 500 m Row → 400 m run. Rest 3 min, repeat 2×
- **Combo 2:** 15 Wall Balls → 25 m Sled Push → 15 Wall Balls → 25 m Sled Push

**Day 3, Interval Run**
- 4 km easy run
- Then: 6×200 m at fast pace with 1 min recovery jog

**Day 4, Station Combos B**
- **Combo 1:** 25 m Sled Pull → 200 m run → 30 m Burpee Broad Jumps → 200 m run. Rest 3 min, repeat 2×
- **Combo 2:** 50 m Farmers Carry → 50 m Sandbag Lunges → 50 m Farmers Carry → 50 m Sandbag Lunges

**Day 1, Long Run**
- 5 km at steady pace. Build to 6 km in week 6.

**Day 2, Half Race Simulation (Stations 1–4)**
- Run 1 km → SkiErg 1,000 m → Run 1 km → Sled Push 50 m → Run 1 km → Sled Pull 50 m → Run 1 km → Burpee Broad Jumps 40 m
- Minimise rest between stations

**Day 3, Speed Work**
- 1 km warm-up
- 4×1 km at target race pace (5:00–5:30/km) with 2 min walk recovery
- 1 km cool-down

**Day 4, Half Race Simulation (Stations 5–8)**
- Run 1 km → Rowing 1,000 m → Run 1 km → Farmers Carry 200 m → Run 1 km → Sandbag Lunges 50 m → Run 1 km → Wall Balls 50 reps

## Nutrition Basics

- **Daily:** Protein at every meal (chicken, fish, eggs, legumes)
- **Pre-workout:** Light carbs 1–2 hours before (banana, toast, oatmeal)
- **Post-workout:** Protein + carbs within 30 minutes
- **Hydration:** 2–3 litres of water daily, more on training days
- **Race week:** Increase carbs slightly, reduce fibre

See the full [Supplement Protocol](/supplements/protocol/) for race-day timing.

## Recommended Supplements for This Program

These three supplements will give you the most benefit during your 8-week build:

- **[Creatine (5g/day)](/supplements/creatine/):** The most evidence-backed supplement for Hyrox. Start now so stores are saturated by race day. Improves power on wall balls, sled work, and carries.
- **[Electrolytes](/supplements/electrolytes/):** Essential during sessions over 60 minutes. Prevents cramps and keeps performance steady through longer station training sets.
- **[Protein powder](/supplements/protein-powder/):** Convenient way to hit your 1.6–2.0g/kg protein target and support muscle recovery between sessions.

See the full [Supplement Protocol](/supplements/protocol/) for exact race-day timing.
