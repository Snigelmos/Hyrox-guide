## Accessories That Make a Difference

Beyond shoes and clothing, the right accessories can save you time, prevent injury, and make your race more comfortable. Here's what's worth considering.

---

## Sports Watches

A GPS watch helps you pace your 1km runs and track your overall time. Essential for executing your pacing strategy.

### Best for Hyrox

- **Garmin Forerunner 265** — GPS, heart rate, excellent for interval tracking, great battery life. Our top pick.
- **COROS PACE 3** — lightweight, accurate GPS, outstanding battery. Best value.
- **Apple Watch Ultra 2** — if you're in the Apple ecosystem. Great display, good GPS.

**Key features to look for:**
- Accurate GPS for indoor use (some venues have GPS issues)
- Lap/interval timer functionality
- Heart rate monitoring
- Quick-glance display (you need to read it while running)

## Hydration

### Running Belt

If you want to carry gels or a small flask, a running belt is the lightest option.

- **FlipBelt Classic** — lies flat against your body, no bounce
- **Naked Running Band** — ultra-minimal, holds gels and phone

### Tips
- Most athletes don't carry hydration during the race — water stations are available
- A belt is mainly useful for carrying 1–2 energy gels

## Energy & Nutrition Accessories

### Energy Gels

Quick carbs mid-race can prevent bonking. Carry 1–2 gels.

- **Maurten Gel 100** — smooth texture, no GI issues for most people
- **SiS GO Isotonic Gel** — no water needed, easy to consume
- **Precision Fuel & Hydration** — great electrolyte + carb combo

### Gel Holders
- Tape gels to the inside of your shorts waistband
- Or use a running belt (see above)
- Practice opening and consuming gels during training

## Grip Accessories

### Lifting Straps / Grips

For the sled pull and farmers carry, grip failure costs minutes.

- **Bear KompleX 3-Hole Grips** — popular in CrossFit, protect palms during rope pull
- **Versa Gripps** — quick-attach lifting straps
- **Liquid Chalk** — improves grip without bulky gloves (check venue rules)

### When to Use
- If grip is your limiter on sled pull or farmers carry, grips help
- Many athletes prefer to train grip strength and race bare-handed
- Practice with whichever you choose — don't try new gear on race day

## Recovery Tools

### Foam Rollers

Essential for training recovery:

- **TriggerPoint GRID** — portable, durable, excellent for quads and back
- **Hyperice Vyper 3** — vibrating roller for deeper tissue work

### Massage Guns

Great for warming up before races and recovery after:

- **Theragun Mini** — portable, powerful enough for pre-race activation
- **Hyperice Hypervolt Go 2** — lightweight, quiet, TSA-approved for travel

### Compression Boots

For serious athletes training 5+ days per week:

- **Normatec 3 Legs** — the gold standard in pneumatic compression
- **Recover from training faster** — especially helpful during high-volume training blocks

## Other Useful Accessories

### Headband / Sweatband
- Keeps sweat out of your eyes
- **Halo Headband** — has a built-in sweat seal
- Any moisture-wicking headband works

### Foam/Grip Tape for Hands
- Athletic tape wrapped around your palms can help with rope pull grip
- Practice taping technique before race day

### Bag
- A gym bag large enough for all your gear, change of clothes, towel, and food
- **Nike Brasilia Duffel** — reliable, affordable, water-resistant bottom
