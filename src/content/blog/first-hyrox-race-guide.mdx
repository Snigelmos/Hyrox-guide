Walking up to your first Hyrox start gate is unlike any other race start. Most first-timers in average gym shape walk away with sub-90 finishes; many others blow up at the burpee broad jumps and crawl through the back half in 1:45 or worse. The difference rarely comes down to fitness — it comes down to knowing what's coming.

Here's the list to read before you run your first one.

## 1. You will go out too fast

This is not a "you might". You will. The starting atmosphere is engineered to make you sprint, music, crowd, lights, the [official Hyrox event format](https://hyrox.com/competition-format/) deliberately stages a high-energy intro. Every first-timer sprints the first 1 km too fast.

What it feels like in practice: that first km lands 30 to 45 seconds quicker than the rest. The cost shows up at Run 5, when your legs feel hollow.

The fix is unglamorous: trust your watch, not your ears. If your goal pace is 5:50/km, your first km should feel slightly disappointing. That's correct.

## 2. The sled weights are real

102 kg (Open Men) and 72 kg (Open Women) on the sled push don't sound like much until you're pushing them across an indoor venue floor after 2 km of running. The published [Hyrox station weights](https://hyrox.com/competition-format/) are the floor of what you'll feel.

Athletes who've trained at race weight push through cleanly. Athletes who haven't routinely come off the sled in 3:20 instead of a planned 2:00. That gap — 80 seconds on a single station — is often 20%+ of the difference between matched first-time finishers.

Train with the real numbers. If your gym doesn't have race-weight sleds, find one before race day, even if it means driving an hour. (See [our home gym guide](/gear/home-gym/) on what alternatives actually transfer.)

## 3. Burpee broad jumps break people

Station 4 is where races split. It's the most metabolically demanding movement in the race, and it lands right before the row, which actively rewards fresh legs.

Most blown races are decided here. Pace it like a hill workout: hard but sustainable. If you're gasping by the 40 m mark, you're going too hard and you'll pay it back, with interest, in the next 25 minutes. The difference between a sub-90 and a 1:50 first race often comes down to whether the athlete held back at burpee 30.

## 4. Transitions eat time

The walk from the end of a run to a station, and from a station back to the running track, takes 30 to 60 seconds each time on a typical Hyrox course. Multiply by 16 transitions and you're at 8 to 16 minutes of dead time, more than the entire SkiErg.

Jog through them. Set up at stations within 5 seconds of arrival. Every second counts. More on this in [our pacing guide](/blog/best-hyrox-pacing-strategy/).

## 5. Grip is everything

Your hands will be the limiter on stations 4 onward. The sled pull, farmers carry, and sandbag lunges all stack grip demand on top of an already-fatigued forearm.

Train grip specifically for at least 6 weeks before your race. Dead hangs, farmers walks at above race weight, hand-over-hand rope pulls. The work is unglamorous and it works.

## 6. The atmosphere is incredible

This isn't a quiet 10 km in the park. Hyrox runs in arenas — large purpose-fitted indoor venues with DJs, LED screens, occasionally fog machines, and a few thousand spectators. It is closer to a sporting event than a fun run. Embrace it; the crowd noise is the only place in fitness where you'll be cheered for finishing 800th.

## 7. You don't need to be "fit enough"

The most common excuse first-timers give themselves: *"I'll sign up when I'm ready."* The truth is that the [Open division](/racing-guide/categories/) is designed for recreational athletes, and the Relay is perfect for absolute beginners. Sign up, then train toward it.

The 12-week build for [sub-90 first-timers](/blog/is-sub-90-hyrox-good/) is genuinely beginner-territory if you've trained 3 days per week for the last year.

## 8. Nutrition matters more than you think

Skipping breakfast, trying a new gel, or undershooting carbs the day before will all bonk you at station 5 or 6 — three of the most predictable race-day mistakes.

The mechanics: Hyrox is 60 to 90 minutes of high glycolytic demand, which means [in-race carbohydrate (around 60 to 90 g/h)](https://www.gssiweb.org/sports-science-exchange/article/sse-217-the-importance-of-carbohydrate-fueling-for-endurance-athletes) maintains pace. The [International Society of Sports Nutrition's position on protein and carb periodisation](https://jissn.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/s12970-018-0242-y) is the closest thing to a standard reference. We summarise the practical version in [our energy gel strategy guide](/blog/energy-gel-strategy-hyrox/).

## 9. The finish line feeling is addictive

Crossing the finish, hearing your name, getting the patch, it is one of the best feelings in fitness. Most first-timers sign up for their next race within a week or two of finishing — the repeat-rate is one of the highest in endurance sport.

## 10. Your second race will be much faster

Now that you know the format, the venue smell, the pacing traps, and your own response to each station, your second Hyrox will typically be 10 to 20 minutes faster. The pattern is clear in [results.hyrox.com](https://results.hyrox.com/) data when you look up athletes' first-vs-second race times: the median improvement on Race 2 with no significant change in training is around 12 minutes for Open Men.

Experience is a massive advantage. Use your first race to learn, not to PR.

## Common questions from first-timers

**Is Hyrox harder than a half marathon?**
Different harder. The cardiovascular load is in the same ballpark, but Hyrox compresses it into 60 to 90 minutes and adds eight strength stations. Most athletes who've done both say Hyrox feels harder, mostly because of the burpee broad jumps and the wall balls at the end.

**Should I train Hyrox-specific or just train hard?**
Hyrox-specific from week 1. The sled push and farmers carry need direct practice; "general fitness" doesn't transfer perfectly. Even 6 weeks of Hyrox-specific work on top of a fitness base typically beats 12 weeks of general training.

**What do I actually wear?**
Compression shorts or short running shorts, a fitted top, the [running shoes you've trained in](/gear/shoes/), and ideally no jewellery. Bring a separate pair of shoes for the warm-up if your race shoes are minimalist.

**How early should I show up on race day?**
At least 90 minutes before your wave. Bag drop, warm-up, station familiarisation, and the wave check-in eat that time fast. Better to sit calmly with your gel and water than to sprint to the start.

**How much does the entry actually cost?**
Open Singles entry sits in the €85 to €150 range depending on city and how early you register. We break down all the hidden costs (travel, gear, gym membership) in our [how much does Hyrox cost](/blog/how-much-does-hyrox-cost/) breakdown.

**What's a realistic goal for my first?**
For Open Men in average gym shape, 1:30 to 1:45 is the meaty middle. Open Women: 1:40 to 2:00. The [time benchmarks page](/times/) breaks this out by age group.
