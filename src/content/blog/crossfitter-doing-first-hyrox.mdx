## Short answer

If you can do Murph, you can finish Hyrox. But you'll probably blow up at station 5.

CrossFit gives you almost everything Hyrox needs — except an aerobic running engine. Most CrossFit-trained athletes finish their first Hyrox in 1:20-1:35, which is solid but well below their potential. Add 6-12 weeks of structured running, and you can be a sub-75 (or even sub-70) athlete with the strength and engine you already have.

## What CrossFit prepares you for

CrossFit transfers exceptionally well to:

- **Wall balls** — same exercise, similar rep schemes (Murph has 100, Karen has 150)
- **Sled push** — assuming you've trained it. Few box programs include it.
- **Burpee broad jumps** — more skill than fitness, but the burpee base is there
- **Rowing** — most CrossFit boxes have rowers; you've done 2k repeats
- **Sandbag work** — if your box programs odd-object lifting
- **Mental toughness** — Hyrox is long but no single rep is hard. CrossFitters thrive on this.

## What CrossFit doesn't prepare you for

The race-killers:

### 1. Running base

Most CrossFit programs include 1-2 km of running per week, max. Hyrox is 8 km. After station 4, you'll feel cardiovascularly fine but your **legs lose pop** — the specific running endurance just isn't there.

The fix: 2-3 dedicated running sessions per week for 6+ weeks before race day. One tempo, one easy long, one easy short.

### 2. Pacing strategy across 70+ minutes

CrossFit WODs are 8-30 minutes. Hyrox is 60-90+. The pacing strategies that work for a 12-minute AMRAP are wildly different from a 75-minute race.

The fix: do at least one full Hyrox simulator before race day. This teaches you what 70 minutes of threshold actually feels like.

### 3. Sled pull (specifically)

Many CrossFit boxes don't have a sled pull setup. The hand-over-hand technique, rope feel, and grip endurance are usually new for crossfitters.

The fix: 2-3 sled pull sessions per week from week 6 onward. If your box doesn't have a setup, find a friend with a heavy gym bag and rope.

### 4. Sandbag carries on shoulders

Most CrossFit programs use sandbag for ground-to-shoulder lifts but not 100m walking lunges with it. The shoulder-loaded lunge under fatigue is brutally specific.

The fix: weekly 4×100 m walking lunges with a 20 kg sandbag (men) or 10 kg (women).

## The 6-week bridge plan from CrossFit to Hyrox

Add these on top of (or modifying) your existing CrossFit program.

### Weeks 1-2: Build run capacity

- **Mon:** Tempo run 4×800 m (5:00 pace for men, 5:30 for women)
- **Wed:** Easy run 6 km Z2
- **Sat:** Long run 10-12 km Z2
- Keep 2-3 days of CrossFit + skill work

### Weeks 3-4: Add specificity

Replace one CrossFit day with a Hyrox-specific brick:

- 1 km run + 25 wall balls + 1 km run + 50 m sled push + 1 km run + farmers carry 200 m

Add weekly sled pull (4 sets at race weight, 2 minutes rest).

### Weeks 5-6: Race prep

- Full Hyrox simulator at 80% effort 4 weeks out
- Final hard simulator 14 days out at 95%
- Cut volume by 40% in final 7 days

## What CrossFit times predict your Hyrox time

Rough mapping (your mileage will vary):

| CrossFit benchmark | Approximate Hyrox time |
|---------------------|------------------------|
| Murph sub-40 | Hyrox sub-65 (with run prep) |
| Helen sub-9 | Hyrox sub-70 (with run prep) |
| Murph 40-50 | Hyrox 70-80 |
| Murph 50-60 | Hyrox 80-90 |
| Murph 60+ | Hyrox 90+ |

These assume you run sub-22 5k. If you run sub-19, drop one tier.
