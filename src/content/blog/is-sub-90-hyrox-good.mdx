## Short answer

**Sub-90 is a strong first-time Hyrox target.** Roughly 60% of Open Men finishers break 90 and ~40% of Open Women — but only about **1 in 3 first-timers** make it. The gap between "fit gym-goer" and "sub-90 Hyrox finisher" is mostly about running base and pacing discipline, not raw strength.

This guide gives you the splits, the realistic training profile, and a 12-week build for first-timers.

## Where sub-90 sits

| Division | Sub-90 percentile |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Open Men** | Top ~60% (above the ~1:30 median) |
| **Open Women** | Top ~30% |
| **First-time Open Men** | Roughly top third |
| **First-time Open Women** | Roughly top quarter |

Wait — that's 92 minutes, not 90. So we need to bank 2-3 minutes earlier:

- Run 1-3 closer to 5:30 pace (you're fresh)
- Sled push under 4:00
- Wall balls in 5:30 instead of 6:00

The takeaway: **sub-90 is tight even for fit first-timers**. Pace is the difference between sub-90 and sub-95.

## Athlete profile (first-timer chasing sub-90)

You'll want:

- **Sub-28 5 km road PR** (men) or sub-31 (women)
- **2 km row in under 9:30**
- **Confident with 100 wall balls broken into 5 sets**
- **Running 25-30 km/week** for 8+ weeks
- **Strength training 2×/week** with at least 1 sled-style session
- **One full Hyrox simulator** completed in training

If you don't have all of these, sub-95 might be a more realistic first-race goal.

## The first-timer's 12-week sub-90 plan

### Weeks 1-4: Build the engine

- 3-4 runs/week — most easy, one quality
- 2 strength days (legs + core focus)
- 1 station-skill session: practice each Hyrox station with technique focus, not race speed

### Weeks 5-8: Add specificity

- Weekly **brick**: 1 km @ 6:00 pace + 25 wall balls + 1 km @ 6:00 + 50 m sled push
- Weekly **race-pace 1 km repeats**: 5×1 km at 5:45, 90 s rest
- Increase weekly running volume to 30-35 km

### Weeks 9-11: Race-specific

- Full Hyrox simulator at 80% effort
- 5 km time trial at week 9 — confirms goal pace
- Add a weekly **wall ball + lunges combo**: 100 wall balls broken + 2×100 m lunges

### Week 12: Taper

- Drop running volume by 50%
- Last hard session 7 days out (3-4 stations only)
- Easy mobility + walking 2 days out

## Sub-90 race-day non-negotiables

1. **Bank time on runs 1-3.** You're fresh — these are your fastest km. Don't waste them at 6:30 pace.
2. **No breaks on the sled push.** Every break = 5-10 seconds. There are no breaks in the sub-90 plan.
3. **Burpees: pace yourself.** First-timers regularly blow up here, taking 9 minutes instead of 7.
4. **Wall balls: break early, break clean.** Do 25 + 25 + 25 + 25, not "go unbroken until you fail at 35."
5. **Fuel before the row.** A gel at km 4 (~28 minutes in) lands in your bloodstream right at the start of station 6.
