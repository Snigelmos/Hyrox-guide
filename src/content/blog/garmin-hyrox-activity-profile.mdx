## Short answer

Garmin has an **official Hyrox activity profile** (jointly developed with Hyrox HQ) available on Connect IQ for all major modern Forerunner, Fenix, and Epix models. It auto-detects stations, captures per-segment splits, and uploads a complete race breakdown to Garmin Connect.

If your watch supports it, install it. If not, this guide covers the manual configuration that gets you 90% of the same data.

## Watches that support the official Hyrox profile

| Model | Supported? |
|-------|------------|
| **Forerunner 165 / 255 / 265 / 955 / 965** | ✅ |
| **Fenix 7 / 7 Pro / 8 / Tactix** | ✅ |
| **Epix Pro / Enduro 3** | ✅ |
| **Vivoactive 5** | ✅ |
| **Forerunner 245 / 645 / 245 Music** | ❌ (custom profile only) |
| **Fenix 5 / 6 / 6 Pro** | ❌ (custom profile only) |

## Installing the official Hyrox profile

1. **Open Connect IQ Store** (in the Garmin Connect app on your phone)
2. **Search "Hyrox"**
3. **Install the official Hyrox app** (developer: Garmin / Hyrox)
4. **Sync your watch** (next time you connect)
5. **On the watch:** Activities & Apps → Add → Hyrox

The profile will appear in your activity menu alongside Run, Bike, etc.

## What the official profile tracks

Once active, the profile auto-detects:

- **Run laps** (1 km each)
- **Station segments** (SkiErg, sled push, sled pull, etc.)
- **Transitions**
- **Total race time**

Your post-race Garmin Connect upload shows a station-by-station breakdown comparable to the official Hyrox results.

## Manual setup for older watches

If your Garmin doesn't support the official profile, build a custom activity instead:

### Step 1: Create a "Hyrox" activity profile

- On the watch: Settings → Activities & Apps → Add New → Other → Rename to "Hyrox"
- Or in Garmin Connect: Devices → Activities & Apps → Edit → Add Custom Activity

### Step 2: Configure data screens

Set up a 4-field primary screen:

| Field | Position |
|-------|----------|
| **Lap Time** | Top |
| **Heart Rate** | Middle-left |
| **Pace** | Middle-right |
| **Total Time** | Bottom |

Add a secondary screen with **distance**, **average HR**, and **kcal**.

### Step 3: Set auto-lap

Best option: **Auto-Lap by Distance, every 1 km.** This gives you a clean run lap every km plus a station lap when you finish station work and start running again.

Alternative: **Manual Lap.** Press the lap button at each station entry and exit. More accurate but easy to forget mid-race.

### Step 4: Disable auto-pause

Auto-pause messes up Hyrox tracking — it will pause every time you stop briefly at a station. **Turn auto-pause off** in the activity settings.

### Step 5: Set HR alert (optional)

Set a high-HR alert at 95% of HRmax. If your watch buzzes, slow down — you're in the zone where most race blow-ups start.

## Connecting a chest strap (recommended)

Wrist HR is **less accurate** during heavy gripping work (sled pull, farmers carry, wall balls). A chest strap (HRM-Pro Plus or HRM-Dual) gives clean data through the entire race.

To pair:

1. Wet the strap electrodes
2. Wear it tight, just below the chest
3. On the watch: Sensors → Add Sensor → HRM
4. Confirm the watch pairs (usually instant)

## Race-day Garmin checklist

The night before:

- ✅ Charge to 100%
- ✅ Sync to phone (latest software)
- ✅ Confirm Hyrox profile is at the top of your activity list
- ✅ Set wrist HR or chest strap as primary

Race morning:

- ✅ Strap on chest HRM after breakfast
- ✅ Watch paired and showing HR before you enter the corral
- ✅ Start activity in the corral (paused) — hit start when the announcer calls "GO"
- ✅ Don't look at the watch for the first 2 km — just run

## After the race

- **Upload to Garmin Connect** within 60 seconds (auto via Bluetooth)
- **View activity** for station-by-station splits
- **Export to Strava** to share publicly (toggle in Garmin Connect settings)
