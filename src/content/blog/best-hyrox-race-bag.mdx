## What a good race bag does

A Hyrox race bag has three jobs:

**1. Carry everything you need to and from the venue,** without making you regret your packing choices.

**2. Survive bag drop.** It will be tossed onto a pile, possibly in rain, with hundreds of other bags. It needs to be findable and durable.

**3. Hold post-race kit.** After the race, your dirty shoes, sweaty kit, and recovery items all need to fit alongside what you brought in. Bags that were full pre-race become problems post-race.

This guide covers the bag types, the top picks, and a complete pack-list.

---

## Bag types compared

### Duffel bags (40–60L)

The classic gym bag. Wide opening for easy access to gear. Carry handles plus a shoulder strap. Most sit upright when packed, making them easy to pack and unpack.

**Pros:** Easy access, fits everything, durable
**Cons:** Single shoulder carry can be uncomfortable for long walks

### Backpacks (25–40L)

Hands-free carrying, good for longer walks from train station to venue. Typical structured running or athletic backpack.

**Pros:** Comfortable to carry, often weather-resistant, hands-free
**Cons:** Smaller capacity, harder to access items quickly

### Hybrid (duffel-backpack)

Bags that convert between carry styles. Often have backpack straps that tuck away.

**Pros:** Best of both worlds
**Cons:** Often heavier, sometimes neither carry mode is fully optimised

### Drawstring bags

Lightweight, simple, and minimal. Good for the post-race walk to the train but limited capacity.

**Pros:** Cheap, lightweight, easy to pack flat
**Cons:** Not durable, not comfortable for heavy loads, no internal organization

For most Hyrox athletes, **a 30–40L duffel or backpack is the sweet spot.** Big enough to hold race kit + post-race kit + recovery items, but not so big you are wandering the venue with luggage.

## Features worth prioritising

### Separate shoe compartment

Bags with a dedicated shoe pocket (often at the end or bottom) keep your dirty post-race shoes from contaminating clean clothes. Highly recommended.

### Waterproof bottom panel

Your bag will sit on wet floors, locker rooms, or rain-soaked grass. A waterproof bottom keeps the contents dry.

### Internal pockets for small items

Bib, timing chip, headphones, gels, and similar small items get lost in a single large compartment. Internal pockets or zippered subdivisions help.

### Bright colour or distinctive markings

At bag drop, hundreds of black bags pile up identically. A bright colour, distinctive print, or attached identifier makes finding yours dramatically faster.

### Comfortable carry

If the venue is more than a 5-minute walk from your transport, comfortable carry matters. Padded shoulder straps for backpacks, padded shoulder strap for duffels.

## The complete Hyrox race-day pack-list

### Must-have

- ✅ Race bib (printed, with safety pins)
- ✅ Timing chip (if separate from bib)
- ✅ Race-day shoes (in shoe compartment if possible)
- ✅ Race shorts / tights
- ✅ Race top
- ✅ Sports bra (if applicable)
- ✅ Race socks
- ✅ Photo ID (for race check-in)
- ✅ Pre-race breakfast (if eating at the venue)
- ✅ Energy gels (1–2)
- ✅ Water bottle
- ✅ Phone + charger
- ✅ Hair ties / headband (if applicable)

### Strongly recommended

- ✅ Compression sleeves (calves, knees as needed)
- ✅ Pre-race warm-up clothing (jacket, joggers — to keep warm before warming up)
- ✅ Foam roller or massage ball (for warm-up)
- ✅ Sunglasses (if outdoor venue)
- ✅ Towel
- ✅ Wet wipes / face wipes
- ✅ Plastic bag for sweaty clothes post-race
- ✅ Post-race clothing (clean and dry — this is more important than you think)
- ✅ Post-race shoes (sandals or recovery shoes)
- ✅ Recovery food / drink for immediately after

### Nice-to-have

- ✅ Headphones (most events do not allow during race, but useful pre- and post-)
- ✅ Backup hair ties
- ✅ Anti-chafe balm (Body Glide or equivalent)
- ✅ Plasters / blister care
- ✅ Anti-inflammatory medication (if you use it)
- ✅ Cash (for parking, food, emergencies)
- ✅ Emergency contact info on paper

### Don't forget for cold/outdoor venues

- ✅ Hat / beanie (to keep warm pre-race)
- ✅ Gloves
- ✅ Extra layer for waiting
- ✅ Hand warmer packets (cheap insurance)

## Packing strategy

### Pack the night before, not race morning

Race morning is for nutrition, hydration, and mental preparation — not packing. Have everything ready the evening before.

### Use compression cubes or zip bags

Separate clean clothes from kit you will wear at the venue. Separate post-race kit from pre-race kit. This prevents the "where is my clean t-shirt" panic when changing post-race.

### Keep the bib accessible

Have your bib in an easily-found location. Many athletes attach it the night before (so they cannot forget it in the bag).

### Pack heavy items low and centred

For backpacks, pack heaviest items (shoes, water bottle) low and close to your back. This prevents the bag from pulling backward and making the carry uncomfortable.

### Keep the post-race clothing dry

Use a separate plastic bag or compression sack for post-race clothing. If your bag is sat on wet ground during the race, the post-race clothes need protection.

## Where to buy

Decathlon offers the best value entry-level options. Specialty outdoor retailers (REI, Cotswold, Snow + Rock) carry mid-range and premium options. Online retailers (Amazon, Wiggle, Sportsshoes.com) offer competitive pricing on most brands.

## Bag-related race-day mistakes

**1. Forgetting the bag drop deadline.** Most events close bag drop 15–30 minutes before your wave. Allow time.

**2. Putting valuables in bag drop.** Bag drop is reasonably secure but not insured. Leave passports, expensive watches, and cash at home or with a non-racing friend.

**3. Bringing too much.** A 60L expedition pack is overkill. The right size is 30–40L for most athletes.

**4. Wearing hard-to-replace items.** Do not wear your only pair of expensive sunglasses if there is a chance they get lost in the bag drop chaos.
