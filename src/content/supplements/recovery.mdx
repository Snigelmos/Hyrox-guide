## Why Recovery Supplements Matter for Hyrox

Training for Hyrox is demanding — you're combining high-volume running with strength work, often 4–6 sessions per week. Your ability to recover between sessions directly determines how consistently you can train, and consistent training is what drives Hyrox performance.

Recovery supplements support your body's natural repair processes:

- **Reduce inflammation** from high-intensity training
- **Improve sleep quality** — where most recovery actually happens
- **Support joint health** through the repetitive impact of running and lunges
- **Maintain immune function** during heavy training blocks
- **Reduce muscle soreness** so you can train again sooner

These supplements are the "nice to have" tier — not as critical as protein, creatine, or electrolytes, but genuinely helpful for athletes training at high volume.

---

## The Key Recovery Supplements

### Omega-3 Fatty Acids (Fish Oil)

**Why:** Omega-3s (EPA and DHA) are potent anti-inflammatories. They reduce exercise-induced muscle soreness, support joint health, and benefit heart and brain function.

**Evidence:** Multiple studies show omega-3 supplementation reduces delayed onset muscle soreness (DOMS) by 15–35% and improves joint mobility.

**Dose:** 2–3g combined EPA + DHA per day, taken with a meal containing fat.

**What to look for:** Triglyceride form (better absorption than ethyl ester), third-party tested for heavy metals, high EPA+DHA per capsule.

---

### Magnesium

**Why:** Magnesium is involved in 300+ enzymatic reactions, including muscle contraction, energy production, and sleep regulation. Many athletes are deficient.

**Evidence:** Magnesium supplementation improves sleep quality, reduces nighttime muscle cramps, and supports overall recovery in physically active adults.

**Dose:** 200–400mg before bed (elemental magnesium).

**Best forms:**
- **Magnesium glycinate** — best absorbed, calming, ideal for sleep
- **Magnesium citrate** — good absorption, mild laxative effect
- **Avoid magnesium oxide** — cheap but poorly absorbed

---

### Tart Cherry Extract

**Why:** Tart cherries are rich in anthocyanins (powerful antioxidants) that reduce inflammation and muscle damage markers after intense exercise.

**Evidence:** Studies show tart cherry juice/extract reduces muscle soreness by 13–25% and speeds strength recovery after heavy training.

**Dose:** 480ml tart cherry juice or 500mg tart cherry extract, twice daily around hard training days.

**When to use:** Most beneficial around your hardest training sessions and during race week recovery.

---

### ZMA (Zinc + Magnesium + B6)

**Why:** ZMA supports sleep quality, hormonal balance, and immune function — all critical during heavy training blocks.

**Evidence:** Athletes with zinc or magnesium deficiencies see significant improvements in sleep quality and recovery with ZMA supplementation.

**Dose:** Follow label directions — typically taken 30–60 minutes before bed on an empty stomach.

**Note:** If you already supplement magnesium separately, you may not need additional ZMA. Choose one or the other to avoid excessive magnesium intake.

## FAQ

**Are recovery supplements worth the money?**
If you're already sleeping 7–9 hours, eating enough protein, and managing training load — yes, they provide a genuine marginal benefit. If those basics aren't dialed in, fix those first.

**Can I get omega-3 from food?**
Yes — 2–3 servings of fatty fish per week (salmon, mackerel, sardines) provides adequate omega-3. Supplement if you don't eat fish regularly.

**Does magnesium really help with sleep?**
For many athletes, yes. Magnesium glycinate in particular has a calming effect. It won't knock you out like a sleep aid, but it supports deeper, more restorative sleep.

**Should I take these year-round?**
Omega-3 and magnesium benefit from consistent daily use. Tart cherry is best used periodically around hard training blocks rather than year-round.

**Can I take all of these together?**
Yes, but avoid taking ZMA and omega-3 together (fat can interfere with ZMA absorption). Take omega-3 with dinner and ZMA/magnesium before bed on an empty stomach.
