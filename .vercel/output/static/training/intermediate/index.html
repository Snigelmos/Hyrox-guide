Intermediate Hyrox Training Program

A 10-week training plan to drop 10–20 minutes off your Hyrox time. Structured running, strength, and race-specific simulation work.

Shave Minutes Off Your Time: 10-Week Plan

You’ve completed at least one Hyrox and want to get faster. This program targets the three pillars of Hyrox performance: running economy, station strength, and race-specific conditioning.

Prerequisites: Can run 10km, comfortable with all 8 stations, first Hyrox completed.

Training schedule: 5 sessions per week (3 running/conditioning, 2 strength/stations)

Day 1 — Easy Run + Core

  • 6–8km easy pace (5:30–6:00/km)
  • Post-run: 3 rounds of 45s plank, 20 dead bugs, 15 Russian twists

Day 2 — Strength A (Push/Legs)

  • Back Squat: 4×8
  • Dumbbell Bench Press: 3×10
  • Walking Lunges: 3×12 each leg (hold dumbbells)
  • Sled Push: 4×25m at race weight
  • Wall Balls: 3×20 reps

Day 3 — Interval Run

  • 2km warm-up
  • 6×800m at 4:15–4:30/km pace with 90s jog recovery
  • 1km cool-down

Day 4 — Strength B (Pull/Carry)

  • Deadlift: 4×6
  • Bent-Over Rows: 3×10
  • Farmers Carry: 4×100m at race weight
  • Sled Pull: 4×25m at race weight
  • SkiErg: 3×500m (focus on power per stroke)

Day 5 — Long Run

  • 10–12km at easy pace
  • Build by 1km each week

Days 6–7 — Rest / active recovery (walk, stretch, foam roll)

Phase 2: Build (Weeks 4–6)

Day 1 — Tempo Run

  • 2km warm-up
  • 4km at threshold pace (comfortably hard — you can say a few words but not hold a conversation)
  • 1km cool-down
  • Build tempo portion to 5km by week 6

Day 2 — Station Power A

  • 1,000m SkiErg (for time — track your split)
  • Rest 3 min
  • Sled Push 50m → immediate 400m run → Sled Pull 50m → 400m run
  • Rest 3 min, repeat 2×
  • 1,000m Row (for time)

Day 3 — Speed Intervals

  • 2km warm-up
  • 8×400m at 3:50–4:00/km pace with 60s walk recovery
  • 1km cool-down

Day 4 — Station Power B

  • Burpee Broad Jumps: 4×20m with 90s rest (focus on speed)
  • Farmers Carry: 3×200m at race weight (for time)
  • Sandbag Lunges: 3×50m at race weight (focus on pace)
  • Wall Balls: 4×25 reps with 60s rest (for time)

Day 5 — Long Run with Surges

  • 12km total
  • Every 2km, surge for 200m at race pace, then return to easy

Days 6–7 — Rest / active recovery

Phase 3: Race-Specific (Weeks 7–9)

Day 1 — Race-Pace Intervals

  • 2km warm-up
  • 8×1km at target race running pace (4:30–5:00/km) with 60s walk
  • 1km cool-down

Day 2 — Half Race Simulation

  • Complete the first 4 rounds (stations 1–4) at race intensity with race weights
  • Run 1km between each station
  • Track your time for each segment

Day 3 — Recovery Run

  • 5km very easy pace

Day 4 — Second Half Simulation

  • Complete rounds 5–8 (stations 5–8) at race intensity with race weights
  • Run 1km between each station
  • Focus on maintaining pace when fatigued

Day 5 — Full Race Simulation (Week 8 only)

  • Complete all 8 rounds at race weights
  • Aim for your target time
  • Week 7 & 9: Replace with a 10km steady run

Days 6–7 — Rest

Phase 4: Taper (Week 10)

Monday — 5km easy run

Tuesday — Light station work: 500m SkiErg, 500m Row, 20 Wall Balls, 100m Farmers Carry (50% effort)

Wednesday — Rest

Thursday — 3km easy run with 4×100m strides

Friday — Rest

Saturday — Walk 20 min, light dynamic stretching

Sunday — RACE DAY 🔥

Key Training Principles

Progressive overload: Each week should be slightly harder than the last — more volume, more intensity, or less rest.

Specificity: Your training should look like the race. Run 1km repeats (not 5km steady), practice stations at race weight, and simulate transitions.

Recovery is training: Your body adapts during rest, not during workouts. Sleep 7–9 hours, manage stress, and don’t skip rest days.

Track everything: Log your station times, running splits, and how you felt. Data drives improvement.

Target Improvements

With consistent training over 10 weeks, expect:

AreaTypical Improvement
Overall time10–20 minutes faster
Running pace15–30 sec/km faster
Weakest station30–90 seconds faster
Transitions2–4 minutes saved total

Recommended Supplements for This Program

At the intermediate level, your training intensity and volume increase significantly. These supplements will support your performance and recovery:

  • Creatine (5g/day) — Non-negotiable at this level. Directly improves power output on every station and speeds recovery between high-intensity sessions.
  • Pre-workout / Caffeine — Use caffeine (150–250mg) on your hardest training days — race simulations, long intervals, and heavy station sessions.
  • Electrolytes — With 5–6 training sessions per week, consistent electrolyte intake prevents cumulative dehydration and reduces cramping.
  • Protein powder — Hit 1.6–2.0g protein per kg bodyweight daily. At this training volume, food alone often falls short without supplementation.
  • Energy gels — Practice your race-day gel strategy in training during sessions lasting 90+ minutes. Never use a new gel on race day.
